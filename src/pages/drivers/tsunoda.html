From Japan to the Global Stage

Born on May 11, 2000, in Kanagawa, Japan, Yuki Tsunoda's journey to Formula One is a testament to his talent and determination. Starting in karting and advancing through the Japanese Formula 4 Championship, where he became champion in 2018, Tsunoda quickly caught the eye of Honda’s Driver Academy. His performances earned him a place in the Red Bull Junior Team, paving the way for his ascent through European single-seater racing, including a standout year in FIA Formula 2 in 2020, where he secured third place in the championship.

Formula One Debut with AlphaTauri

Tsunoda made his Formula One debut with Scuderia AlphaTauri in 2021, becoming the first Japanese driver on the grid since Kamui Kobayashi in 2014. His aggressive driving style and ability to adapt quickly to the challenges of Formula One showcased his raw talent. Tsunoda scored points in his very first race, making an immediate impact. Over the seasons, he has developed into a more consistent and mature driver, contributing valuable performances to the team.

The Future for Tsunoda

With his youthful energy and relentless determination, Yuki Tsunoda remains a promising talent in Formula One. Backed by Red Bull and Honda, he is poised to continue honing his skills and delivering strong results for AlphaTauri. As he gains more experience, Tsunoda is seen as a key figure in Japan’s resurgence in the sport, inspiring the next generation of drivers from his home country.
