A Strong Racing Heritage

Born on September 1, 1994, in Madrid, Spain, Carlos Sainz inherited his passion for motorsport from his father, two-time World Rally Champion Carlos Sainz Sr. His karting successes led to a swift rise through the junior formulae, with standout performances in Formula Renault, GP3, and Formula Renault 3.5. Sainz made his Formula One debut with Toro Rosso in 2015, where he impressed with his consistency and racecraft.

Journey Through the Grid

After stints with Renault and McLaren, Sainz earned a reputation as one of the most adaptable and reliable drivers on the grid. His move to McLaren in 2019 marked a turning point, as he secured his first podium in Brazil that year and consistently delivered strong performances. In 2021, Sainz joined Scuderia Ferrari, seamlessly integrating into the team and scoring multiple podiums in his debut season.

The Ferrari Dream

Carlos Sainz’s partnership with Ferrari has showcased his strategic brilliance and calm under pressure, earning him the nickname "The Smooth Operator." His maiden F1 victory at the 2022 British Grand Prix was a testament to his determination and skill. As he continues to grow with the team, Sainz remains committed to chasing further wins and helping Ferrari return to championship glory.
