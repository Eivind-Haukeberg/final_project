Breaking New Ground

Born on May 30, 1999, in Shanghai, China, Zhou Guanyu made history as the first Chinese driver to race in Formula One. Zhou’s journey began in karting, where he displayed exceptional talent, earning a spot in Ferrari's Driver Academy in 2014. After moving to the Renault Sport Academy in 2019, Zhou climbed the ranks of single-seater racing, finishing as vice-champion in the 2021 FIA Formula 2 Championship. His strong performances earned him a place with Alfa Romeo for the 2022 Formula One season.

Impressive Debut

Zhou debuted in Formula One at the 2022 Bahrain Grand Prix, securing points in his very first race—a feat that underscored his potential. Throughout his rookie season, he displayed maturity and skill, often challenging his more experienced teammate, Valtteri Bottas. Zhou’s calm approach under pressure and ability to adapt to Formula One's demanding environment have established him as a promising talent.

A Trailblazer for China

As China’s first Formula One driver, Zhou is a trailblazer and a source of immense pride for his nation. His presence on the grid has opened the door for greater interest in motorsport in China, inspiring the next generation of racers. Zhou’s journey, marked by determination and hard work, continues to make waves in the world of Formula One.
