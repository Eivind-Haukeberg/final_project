Ascent to F1

Born on October 16, 1997, in Monte Carlo, Monaco, Charles Leclerc showed early promise as a racing prodigy. He dominated karting championships before progressing through single-seaters, winning the GP3 Series in 2016 and the Formula 2 Championship in 2017. These successes paved the way for his Formula One debut with Alfa Romeo Sauber in 2018, where he quickly made an impression with his speed and maturity.

Ferrari and Early Triumphs

Leclerc joined Scuderia Ferrari in 2019, becoming the youngest driver to race for the iconic team in over 50 years. In his debut season, he claimed two sensational victories at Spa-Francorchamps and Monza, cementing his status as Ferrari's rising star. Known for his fearless overtakes and exceptional qualifying performances, Leclerc has been instrumental in revitalizing Ferrari's competitiveness.

Determined to Deliver

As Ferrari's lead driver, Leclerc remains focused on bringing the championship back to Maranello. Combining raw speed with a deep understanding of racecraft, he continues to push the limits of his SF-23 car. With a strong following and undeniable talent, Leclerc is poised to achieve greatness and deliver on the legacy of Ferrari legends before him.
