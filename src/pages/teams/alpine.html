The Renault Legacy

Alpine F1 Team traces its origins back to Renault’s rich history in motorsport, dating to the 1970s. Renault entered Formula One in 1977, quickly establishing itself as a pioneer in turbocharged engines. Over the decades, Renault achieved immense success, powering championship-winning cars for legendary drivers like Alain Prost and Fernando Alonso. The Renault team transitioned into Alpine in 2021, named after Renault’s iconic performance brand. This change signaled a new chapter, highlighting a commitment to modernity, style, and success in Formula One.

A Fresh Identity

The rebranding to Alpine brought a fresh look to the grid, with the team adopting a striking blue livery inspired by the French flag. Alpine entered the 2021 season with an experienced driver lineup of two-time world champion Fernando Alonso and French talent Esteban Ocon. One of the team’s defining moments came during the 2021 Hungarian Grand Prix, where Ocon secured his maiden Formula One victory. Alonso’s masterclass in defense against Lewis Hamilton in that race underscored Alpine’s ability to punch above its weight, earning admiration from fans and competitors alike.

Engineering Excellence

Operating out of Enstone, England, and Viry-Châtillon, France, Alpine combines world-class chassis development with innovative engine design. The team’s hybrid power units are a testament to Renault’s engineering expertise, balancing performance and efficiency. With significant investments in infrastructure and a focus on data-driven strategies, Alpine aims to challenge the top teams on the grid.

The Road Ahead

Alpine’s ambition is clear: to become a consistent frontrunner in Formula One. With Esteban Ocon and Pierre Gasly as their current driver lineup, the team is blending homegrown French talent with global engineering excellence. Alpine’s commitment to sustainability and pushing technological boundaries positions it as a team prepared to thrive in the future of Formula One.
