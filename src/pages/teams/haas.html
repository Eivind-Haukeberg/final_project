A Bold Entry

Haas F1 Team was founded in 2014 by Gene Haas, a successful entrepreneur and owner of Haas Automation, a leading CNC machine tool manufacturer. The team officially entered Formula One in 2016, breaking a 30-year absence of American teams from the sport. Haas adopted an innovative model, collaborating with Ferrari for power units, technical support, and components, allowing it to focus resources on other competitive areas. Haas’ debut season was impressive, with Romain Grosjean scoring points in their very first race at the Australian Grand Prix. The team’s strong performance throughout the 2016 season marked a remarkable entry into the highly competitive world of Formula One.

Challenges and Resilience

After a promising start, Haas faced challenges in maintaining consistency. Budget constraints and technical difficulties often hindered the team’s progress. However, Haas demonstrated resilience, securing its best Constructors' Championship result of fifth place in 2018, thanks to strong performances by Grosjean and Kevin Magnussen. The team experienced setbacks during the 2019 and 2020 seasons, struggling with car performance and reliability. Despite these hurdles, Haas remained committed to its vision of competing at the highest level, rebuilding its foundation for future success.

A New Chapter

In 2021, Haas underwent a significant transformation, bringing in a young driver lineup of Mick Schumacher and Nikita Mazepin. The focus shifted toward long-term growth, with the team opting to invest in development for future seasons. In 2023, experienced drivers Nico Hülkenberg and Kevin Magnussen returned to provide valuable experience and leadership, signaling Haas’ intent to climb back into midfield contention. Operating out of Kannapolis, North Carolina, Haas remains committed to its unique business model, combining cost-efficiency with competitive ambition. The team’s collaboration with Ferrari continues to provide technical expertise, ensuring Haas remains a steady presence on the grid.

Looking Ahead

Haas F1 Team is poised to continue its journey in Formula One, striving for improved results and a consistent presence in the midfield. With an experienced driver lineup, strategic partnerships, and a resilient team culture, Haas is determined to showcase the fighting spirit of American motorsport.
