The Sauber Legacy

Kick Sauber’s origins lie in Sauber Motorsport, a team founded in 1970 by Peter Sauber in Hinwil, Switzerland. Originally competing in endurance racing, Sauber transitioned to Formula One in 1993, quickly earning respect for its technical ingenuity and competitive spirit. The team’s collaboration with engine suppliers like Mercedes and BMW, along with its ability to develop talent, cemented Sauber as a mainstay on the grid. Over the years, Sauber nurtured future champions like Kimi Räikkönen and Sebastian Vettel, showcasing its role as a vital part of Formula One’s ecosystem. Its dedication to innovation and development paved the way for the team’s evolution into Kick Sauber.

The Kick Rebrand

In 2024, the team rebranded as Kick Sauber, embracing a bold new vision driven by its partnership with Kick, a dynamic global brand. The rebrand reflects a modern approach to racing while maintaining the engineering prowess and team culture that Sauber established over decades. Kick Sauber aims to merge its historic foundation with the vibrancy and ambition of a new era. With drivers Zhou Guanyu and Valtteri Bottas leading the charge, Kick Sauber combines youthful energy with seasoned experience. This balance positions the team as a formidable competitor in the ever-competitive midfield battle.

Technical Innovation and Swiss Precision

Operating out of its state-of-the-art facility in Hinwil, Switzerland, Kick Sauber embodies precision and innovation. The team’s commitment to aerodynamics, chassis development, and hybrid power units ensures it remains competitive on the grid. Strategic collaborations and a focus on sustainability underscore the team’s forward-thinking approach to racing. Kick Sauber’s emphasis on data-driven strategies and advanced simulation technology allows it to adapt quickly to evolving regulations and challenges, ensuring its continued progress in Formula One.

A Vision for the Future

Kick Sauber is on the verge of another major transformation. In 2026, the team will become Audi’s official works team, marking a new chapter in its illustrious history. Audi’s entry into Formula One represents a significant investment in sustainable hybrid technology, advanced engineering, and competitive excellence. This transition promises to elevate Kick Sauber to new heights, as the team takes on the challenge of competing at the front of the grid. With significant investments in development, a robust driver lineup, and a dedication to nurturing talent, Kick Sauber is well-equipped for this journey. The combination of Sauber’s precision, Kick’s dynamic vision, and Audi’s engineering expertise sets the stage for an exciting future.
