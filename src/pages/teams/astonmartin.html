A Storied Brand with Racing Roots

Aston Martin’s history in motorsport dates back to the 1920s, where the brand first competed in endurance racing. The luxury British manufacturer made its Formula One debut in 1959, though the campaign was short-lived. Fast forward to 2021, and Aston Martin officially returned to Formula One, reviving its legacy under the stewardship of Lawrence Stroll. This marked the transformation of Racing Point into Aston Martin F1 Team, blending a storied heritage with cutting-edge innovation.

Modern Renaissance

The rebranded Aston Martin F1 Team entered the 2021 season with a renewed sense of purpose. Boasting drivers like four-time world champion Sebastian Vettel and Canadian talent Lance Stroll, the team aimed to compete at the highest level. The AMR21, Aston Martin’s first car of its new era, showcased a commitment to performance and engineering brilliance, capturing podium finishes and building a strong foundation for the future. In 2023, Fernando Alonso joined the team, bringing his experience and fierce determination to Aston Martin. His podium finishes early in the season reignited the team’s momentum and inspired its growing fanbase.

Engineering Brilliance and Ambition

Aston Martin’s state-of-the-art headquarters in Silverstone, England, serves as a hub for innovation. With investments in advanced facilities and a focus on aerodynamics and power unit optimization, the team is determined to challenge Formula One’s dominant forces. Aston Martin’s collaboration with Mercedes for engines and technical support has further bolstered its competitiveness on the grid.

Vision for the Future

Aston Martin Formula One Team is on a mission to climb to the top of the Constructors' Championship. With a focus on sustainability, cutting-edge engineering, and a blend of experienced drivers and young talent, Aston Martin is poised to redefine success in Formula One. The team’s passion for excellence, coupled with its rich heritage, ensures a bright and exciting future.
